How Trusts Are Taxed in General

Trusts in the United States are divided into two main types for taxation: grantor and non-grantor trusts. Grantor trusts have their income taxed directly to the grantor under individual tax rates, while non-grantor trusts are separate entities and file their own returns. In the case of a non-grantor trust, retained income is taxed to the trust itself. This distinction matters because trusts reach the highest tax brackets much faster than individuals, meaning a small amount of income can bring about a large tax burden.

The 2025 trust tax brackets apply specifically to the taxable income of non-grantor trusts. Because of compressed brackets, even modest income amounts can push a trust into higher tax rates, increasing the total liability owed to both federal and state governments.
